## Description

The University of Bristol Botanic Garden is a botanical garden in Bristol, England. The garden moved to its current site in Stoke Bishop in 2005, having previously been at two other sites in the city. The garden is home to impressive collections of some 4,500 plant species from over 200 plant families within its five-acre site.

**Opening Hours:** Daily 10am-4:30pm (main season); 10am to dusk Monday-Friday (late Nov-early March, closed weekends)

**Admission:** Adults £9 (Gift Aid £9.90), Free for children, students, University of Bristol staff/alumni. Wednesdays are 'DAISY' day - donate as it suits you. Card payments only.

**What to See:**
- Evolutionary Dell with ginkgos, cycads, tree ferns and the Wollemi Pine
- Chinese and Western Medicinal Herb Gardens
- Mediterranean collection showcasing plants from Mediterranean climates
- Large glasshouses spanning four different climates including tropical and subtropical zones
- Floral Diversity Collection showing extraordinary range of flower forms
- Tropical fruits, medicinal plants, succulents, orchids and giant Amazon waterlily
