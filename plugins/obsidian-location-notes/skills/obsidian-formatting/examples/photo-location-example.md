## Description

The Eiffel Tower is an iconic iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. Built in 1889, it has become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognizable structures in the world. The tower stands 330 meters tall and offers spectacular views of Paris from its observation decks.

## Photography Tips

**Best times for photography:**
- Golden hour (1 hour after sunrise, 1 hour before sunset)
- Blue hour (twilight)
- Night (illuminated tower)

**Recommended viewpoints:**
- Trocadéro Gardens (classic frontal view)
- Champ de Mars (from base looking up)
- Bir-Hakeim Bridge (unique angle with bridge in foreground)
- Seine River banks (reflections)

**Camera settings:**
- Use tripod for long exposures at night
- Wide-angle lens (16-35mm) for full tower shots
- Telephoto lens (70-200mm) for detail shots
- Use ND filter for daytime long exposures

## Travel Information

**Getting there:**
- Metro: Line 6 (Bir-Hakeim), Line 9 (Trocadéro)
- RER: Line C (Champ de Mars - Tour Eiffel)
- Bus: Lines 42, 69, 82, 87

**Parking:**
- Limited street parking nearby
- Parking Pullman Tour Eiffel (22 Avenue de Suffren)
- Recommend using public transportation

**Access:**
- Open year-round
- Best to visit early morning or late evening for fewer crowds
- Security screening required
