# Country Sections Reference

This document defines the 5 Travel Information sub-sections for country notes. Each section specifies what to research and how to structure the output.

---

## Best Time to Visit

**Research:**
- Seasons (wet/dry, summer/winter) and their typical temperature ranges
- Rainfall patterns by month
- Peak tourist season: when it falls, pros (full services, events) and cons (crowds, prices)
- Off-peak season benefits (lower prices, fewer crowds, quieter experiences)
- Major festivals and public holidays by month that may affect travel

**Structure:**
2-3 sentence overview of the general climate and recommended timing, followed by bullet points organised by season or month covering conditions and notable events.

---

## Visa Requirements

**Research:**
- Visa requirements specifically for UK, EU, and US passport holders
- Visa types available: tourist visa, e-visa, visa on arrival, visa-free entry
- Application process: where to apply (embassy, online portal, on arrival)
- Permitted duration of stay
- Visa cost (approximate)
- Passport validity requirements (e.g. "must be valid for 6 months beyond stay")

**Structure:**
Lead with the most common traveller scenario (typically UK/EU/US). Add specifics for each passport type where they differ. Note clearly if requirements vary significantly by nationality and link to the official visa portal under Useful Links.

---

## Currency & Prices

**Research:**
- Local currency name and ISO code
- Approximate exchange rates against GBP, EUR, USD
- Credit card acceptance: widespread, limited, or cash-only in most situations
- ATM availability: cities vs rural areas
- Tipping norms: restaurants, taxis, hotels (percentage or flat amount, and whether it is expected or unusual)
- Rough cost guide for:
  - Budget meal (street food or cheap local restaurant)
  - Mid-range restaurant meal for one
  - Coffee (café)
  - Local transport (bus, metro, tuk-tuk per journey)
  - Budget accommodation per night
  - Mid-range accommodation per night

**Structure:**
Currency overview (name, code, exchange rate notes, card/ATM situation), then tipping norms in 2-3 sentences, then a rough price guide as a short bullet list or table.

---

## Safety

**Research:**
- Overall safety rating from FCDO (UK) and/or US State Department travel advisory
- Specific regions or areas to avoid, if any
- Common scams targeting tourists (e.g. taxi overcharging, fake tours, distraction theft)
- Petty crime risk (pickpocketing, bag snatching)
- Emergency numbers: police, ambulance, tourist police (if applicable)
- Health considerations:
  - Recommended vaccinations (check NHS Fit for Travel / CDC)
  - Tap water safety
  - Quality and availability of medical facilities

**Structure:**
Start with an overall assessment (e.g. "Generally safe for tourists" or "Exercise high caution"). Follow with specific watch-outs as a bullet list covering scams, crime, health, and emergency contacts.

---

## Airport

**Research:**
- Main international airport(s): full name, IATA code, distance from city centre
- Transport options from each airport to the city centre:
  - Metro/rail: line name, journey time, approximate cost
  - Bus: service name or number, journey time, approximate cost
  - Taxi/ride-hail: typical fare range, journey time, tips for avoiding overcharging
  - Private transfer: typical cost range

**Structure:**
One entry per major international airport. Under each airport, list transport options as bullets with time and cost for each. Note any options that are particularly recommended or ones to avoid.
